# Information Security Program Charter
**Company:** CloudPay India Pvt Ltd
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 31 March 2026
**Written by:** CISO
**Approved by:** CEO

## 1. Why does this program exist?
CloudPay India handles payment data for thousands 
of Indian businesses. A security breach would 
destroy customer trust, trigger regulatory fines, 
and shut down the business. This security program 
exists to prevent that from happening.

## 2. Who does this apply to?
Every employee, contractor, and vendor who touches 
CloudPay India systems or data.

## 3. What are we trying to achieve?
- Protect customer payment data and personal information
- Pass our SOC 2 Type II audit
- Comply with DPDPA 2023
- Make sure a security incident never destroys the business

## 4. How much risk will we accept?
CloudPay India will NOT accept any risk that could 
expose customer payment data. We have zero tolerance 
for data breaches. We accept medium tolerance for 
internal operational risks where backup plans exist.

## 5. Who is responsible for what?

| Person | Their security responsibility |
|---|---|
| CEO | Approves security budget. Ultimate accountability |
| CISO | Runs the security program day to day |
| IT Manager | Puts technical controls in place |
| All Employees | Follow all security policies. Report incidents |
| Legal Team | Manages our compliance with Indian laws |

## 6. How do we check the program is working?
The Security Steering Committee — CEO, CISO, CTO, 
Legal — meets every 3 months to review:
- Any security incidents that happened
- Audit results
- Current risks
- Budget needs

## 7. When is this reviewed?
Every year. Or immediately after a major security 
incident or new law that affects CloudPay.

## 8. Documents that sit under this charter
- Information Security Policy
- Acceptable Use Policy
- Access Control Policy
- Incident Response Policy
